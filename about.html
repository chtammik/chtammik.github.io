About


I am a Sound Designer, with a passion for game audio - that magic place where sound design meets code. I am experienced in Audiokinetic's Wwise and Firelight's Fmod Studio Middleware and I have worked with both the Unity and Unreal Game Engines.

I am also knowledgeable in music composition (interactive music) as well as all areas of the film production cycle including Production Sound & ADR Recording, Sound Effects Editing, Sound Design, Foley, Surround-Sound Mixing & delivery procedures.

To see some of my work feel free to check out my portfolio.

My sound career started with my a classical training in playing the Clarinet. During high school I discovered my passion for electronic music and after dabbling in DJ-ing I soon started developing my skills in music production and sound design. After working in advertising for two and a half years I began to focus my intentions toward post production for film and television.

My studies at Vancouver Film School introduced me to game audio in which I found my true passion. This became more and more obvious as I participated in numerous game jams collaborating and developing fully functional games with limited resources and a harsh deadline. I thrive on the interaction with other human beings within a team spirit.

When I first arrived in Vancouver the thing that fascinated me the most was the sense of community with in the game development and audio world. Specifically the Full Indie and Vancouver Sound Designers Meetup groups have forged a welcoming enviroment that is fun to participate in. If you are in Vancouver come meet up with us!

On my Soundcloud profile I play with musical ideas, work with sound in new ways and dive deep into the possibilities of Digital Signal Processing and Synthesis. It also serves as a platform for music I write at game jams, sound-scapes and other miscellaneous sound projects I work on.

Reaper

I am a big fan of the audio software REAPER by Cockos Inc. This has a couple of reasons:

Efficiency: It runs on an extremely low memory/CPU footprint so I can run it on any machine. It is the most stable enviroment when working with plugin effects and starts extremely fast (seconds as opposed to minutes).

Customisation: I can change the layout, define custom actions, optimize menues and even add my own functionality via scripting. This enables me to automate my workflow doing less maintainace and focusing on whats really important. The actual sound design.

I have set up a repository of the scripts I have written for Reaper thats available on GitHub and it is fully compatible with the ReaPack package manager available here.
